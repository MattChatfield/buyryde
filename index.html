About Ryde

This is a rare opportunity to acquire a former salon in the beating heart of the Isle of Wight’s largest town and long-established commercial centre. Ryde benefits from strong local trade plus a seasonal influx of visitors arriving by fast catamaran and hovercraft, which boosts footfall along the High Street, Union Street and the Esplanade.

The town is seeing heritage-led regeneration to enhance its historic core and make the high street more attractive to retail, leisure and hospitality businesses. Nationally-funded initiatives targeted at improving shopfronts, restoring buildings and upgrading the public realm are helping to create an environment where new businesses can thrive.

Ryde’s retail mix — national names alongside a lively independent sector of boutiques, cafés and specialist shops — gives a town-centre unit excellent potential whether you’re planning a retail store, café/restaurant or mixed-use business with first-floor views. For a broader view of Ryde as a shopping and visitor destination, see this overview: Isle of Wight — shopping & visitor guide.

In short: a centrally located, flexible premises in Ryde offers both immediate trading potential and the opportunity to benefit from ongoing town-centre improvements — an attractive proposition for investors, business owners and entrepreneurs alike.
